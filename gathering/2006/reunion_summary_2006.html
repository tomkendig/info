Summary of 2006 Kendig Gathering

      9th of July 2006

    The gathering this year was more festive than previous years as it was a combined reunion and celebration of Kenny and Toots (Kendig) Lichty's 50th Wedding Anniversary. There were approximately 120 people in attendance including 63 people with ties to the Kendig surname - the balance of the folks were in attendance to honor Kenny and Toot's Anniversary.

    The weather was sunny and comfortably warm with temperatures in the mid 70s. Many brought their lawn chairs and sat in the shade of the large trees around the pavilion at East Lampeter Community park off Hobson Road just east of Lancaster.

    The food this year was exceptional. The sandwiches and wraps and relish trays were catered for the Anniversary. The drinks were also provided by Aunt Toots. Ray Kendig fired up the propane heated garbage can to steam fresh corn - delicious. Most people brought covered dishes, excellent covered dishes. Deviled eggs, red beet eggs,  bean salad, cooked beans. Desserts too made the event complete with Yum Yum, pecan pie, shoe fly pie, apple pie, several fruit salads - a complete spread of the bounties of Lancaster County. Kenny and Toots children (Mike and Teri and Tim) helped with much of the setup and tear down.

    The person that traveled  furthest would have been Tom Kendig from California, 2840 miles. Second farthest would be Craig & Mary Ellen Butz from Rochester New York, 319 miles. Linda Lowry from Salisbury Maryland, 162 miles also attended. The most distant in terms of relation from Tom Kendig was a new attender, Elizabeth Kendig who is from Pittsburgh PA and is attending Franklin & Marshall college in Lancaster. She and her cousin Alex stopped by and enjoyed the food and people. After some research we calculated she and Tom Kendig are 5th cousins. Other more distant relatives but not directly Kendigs attending included Don and Bonnie Mancuso and Phyllis and Scott Drackley whose parents Lillian and Stoney recently passed away. Local Mennonite historian Joanne Hess Siegrist stopped by to report on her trip to Switzerland and the upcoming reconciliation project in the town of Emmental Switzerland called the Taeuferjahr. It turns out Gene Lowry's wife Mary is good friends with Joanne and it was through Mary that the lineage of Jane Evans Best may be traced. Jane Evans Best is a publisher of one of the key Kendig history articles in the Pennsylvania Mennonite Heritage Journal.

    There were two antique autos that made for great photo opportunities. Aunt Toots provided bubbles for the kids to play with. Jay Kendig provided a TV so the World Cup final could be watched. Italy beat France after a tied match of 1-1 went to kicks from the penalty mark.

    This year there was little interest in the Genealogy share or Kendig tour. Maybe in two more years there will be more interest. Several of us did go the following day to the beautiful residence of Mary and Gene Lowry south of New Holland to visit and share how Mary does genealogy work. Mary is kind of the secretary for the annual Rank reunion which has fliers and mailing lists. Some of Mary's tips on how to record historical documents are published on the Kendig yahoo group which focuses on Kendig genealogy.

    Plans remain for a 2008 Kendig gathering which will be held on the second Sunday of July, the 13th. Details for the gathering can be found at www.kendig.info and at the KendigGathering yahoo group (which has some pictures recalling the event). Keep in touch, talk it up and we'll see you there!
